Wave Terrain Synth

How the sound is made

The terrain is a 2D surface, height = f(x, z). A small orbit circles around on top of it, once per audio period; the height it passes over becomes the waveform. Frequency is how fast the orbit goes around. FM Ratio/Intensity wobbles that speed. Wave picks the terrain shape, A reshapes it, Y-Scale stretches its height.

Modulation matrix

Rows are sources (LFO1, LFO2, Envelope) — signals that move on their own. Columns are destinations — the parameters they can drive. Drag a cell upward to route a source into a destination; the higher you drag, the stronger the effect. Drag back to the bottom to remove the route. Bypass mutes every route at once without forgetting their depths.

Envelope & LFOs

The ADSR envelope shapes each note: Attack/Decay play once when a note starts, Sustain holds while it's held, Release fades it out after. The two LFOs are steady repeating waves (sine/triangle/square) at their own rate — both are just modulation sources, so route either into anything through the matrix above.

Presets & MIDI

Save/load full setups (including routing) by name, or export/import as a file. Any MIDI controller's notes play the synth and its knobs can be mapped to any control — click MIDI MAP, then touch a knob and move the physical control to link them.
